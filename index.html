Global Nighttime Lights Trend

Time series nighttime light is a reflection of regional economic prosperity/recession and poverty. In this project, we compute a linear fit over the time series of values at each pixel, visualizing the y-intercept in green and positive/negative slopes as red/blue. This graph allows us to see the trends in nighttime lights worldwide nearly 30 years, from 1991 to 2014. The green areas were relative bright in 1991, while the blue and red areas show the decline and rise of nighttime lights.

Source: DMSP OLS Dataset
